Hello there, and welcome to my homepage!

I’m Moeen Mostafavi, I work at the Virginia Retirement System (VRS) applying machine learning and large-scale data analysis to mortgage-backed securities and fixed-income research. My academic work sits at the intersection of NLP, machine learning, and computational social science. I earned my Ph.D. in Systems & Information Engineering at the University of Virginia, where my dissertation on emotion understanding in messaging integrated transformer models with Affect Control Theory and led to a paper in Sociological Methodology (2024).

Along the way, I’ve collaborated across sociology, economics, kinesiology, nursing, political science, data science, business, and engineering on projects ranging from telematics-based fleet safety (recognized by NAFA as a “Best Special Project in North America”) to RFID + haptics for collision prediction and technology-enabled sleep tracking in older adults with MCI.

If you’re new here, the best places to start are: About (short bio & highlights) and Projects (selected work with links). You can also find more on Google Scholar and LinkedIn.
